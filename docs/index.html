Multiplier vs. K=1 Log/LNS Spike Detector

Two RTL designs of the same function spike = (A·B + C·D) > Vth (12-bit inputs) — an exact-multiplier baseline and a multiplier-free log-domain (LNS, K=1) variant — synthesized and compared on the sky130 HD standard-cell library.

Dropping the two multipliers for the K=1 log detector cuts area 70% and estimated power 74%, at a 5.60% accuracy cost vs. exact math.

Why it shrinks — cell-area mix

The multiplier spends most of its area on adder cells (xor/maj arrays); the log design replaces them with converter logic () and a small ROM/mux. Both carry the identical registered-I/O flip-flops () — the multiplier just wraps them in far more combinational area.

K=1 accuracy cost — disagreement vs exact

Over 4 M Monte-Carlo samples × each threshold (the 12-bit space, 4096⁴ ≈ 2.8×10¹⁴, cannot be enumerated, so this is a sampled estimate). Design 2's RTL is bit-exact to its K=1 model; a disagreement with exact math is the approximation cost, not a bug. Overall = 5.60%, peaking at mid-range thresholds and vanishing at the extremes.

Flow: Python golden model (emits the F-ROM) → RTL (Verilog) → Icarus verify (63,875 vectors, both PASS) → yosys synth to sky130 HD → analytic power + standard-cell floorplan. Reproduce: ./run.sh then python3 model/floorplan.py && python3 model/build_page.py.

Estimates are labelled as such. Routed area/power/timing would come from OpenROAD/OpenLane P&R + OpenSTA; the comparison ratios reported here are the robust figures.
